Mechanics

Scalar:

A scalar quantity has magnitude only; it has no direction E.g. mass, time, volume

Vector:

A vector quantity has both a magnitude and direction E.g. Force, Velocity

Distance:

The length from one point to another. Distance is measured in metres. It does not take direction in account. (Distance is scalar)

Displacement (S):

is the distance in a certain direction. (Vector)

Velocity (U or V):

is the rate of change of displacement. (Vector) (M/s)

(U = Initial Velocity, V = Final Velocity)

Acceleration:

is the rate of change of velocity. (Vector) (M/s/s) or ms-2

The Equations of Motion

1. V = U + at

2. S = Ut + 1⁄2at2

3. V2 = U2 + 2aS

How to use the equations of motion to solve problems

1. List the three equations.

2. List the information given.

3. Standardise the units in the information given.

4. Use the equation with the only one unknown. This will give the required answer, or else information necessary to get the answer using another of the equations.



Vertical Motion Under Gravity:

A body falling freely under gravity, experiences a uniform acceleration (g) towards the centre of the earth. The value of this acceleration is usually taken as g = 9.8ms2

Mass (m):

The mass of a body is the quantity of the matter in it, and is constant for a body under all conditions, except at speeds approaching the speed of light. Mass is measured in kilograms (kg). Mass is scalar.

Momentum (p):

Momentum is the quantity of mention in the body. It is the product of mass and velocity (mv) of the body. Momentum is a vector. It has no special unit, Kg/ ms-1.

Force (F):

is that which tends to give a body motion, or change its existing motion, in magnitude or in direction.

The Laws of Motion:
  • Law 1

    A body will remain in a state of rest, or travelling with a constant velocity unless an external force acts on it

  • Law 2

    When a force acts on a body the rate of change of the bodys momentum is directly proportional to the force and is in the direction of the force

  • Law 3

    To every action there is an equal and opposite reaction



The Principle of Conservation of Motion

In a system of colliding bodies, the total momenta of all the bodies remains constant provided no external force acts.

Weight:

is the force exerted on a body by the gravity of the earth.

Work:

work is done when a force moves its point of application. W = F.s (Work = force X distance moved, in the direction of the force) The unit of work is the “joule”

Energy:

is the ability to do work or move something

Kinetic:

Energy a body has because it is moving.

K.E. = 1⁄2(mass) . (velocity)2 = 1⁄2mv2

Potential:

Energy a body has because of its configuration or position. E.g. on top of a cliff.

P.E. = mgh

Types of Energy
  • Kinetic
  • Potential
  • Heat
  • Electrical
  • Magnetic
  • Sound
  • Light
  • Chemical
Newton’s Universal law of Gravitation:

“Any two bodies in the universe attract each other with a force which is directly proportional to the product of their masses, and is inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them”

F = G m1 m2 / s2

    Where:
  • F = the force of attraction
  • M1 = the 1st mass
  • M2 = the 2nd mass
  • S = the distance between the centre of the two masses
  • G = a constant, 6.67 x 10 -11 Nm2 kg -2
