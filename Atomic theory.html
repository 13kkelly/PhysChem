Atomic Structure

Chemical Symbol:

How an element is represented on the Periodic Table. (Always with a capital or a captial and a small

Atomic Number:

Is the number of protons in an atom

Mass Number:

is the numver of protons and neurons in an atom (usually a decimal) The unit of mass is a.m.u.(Atomic Mass Unit)

Relative Atomic Mass:

The mass of an atom compared to 1 / 12th of the mass of an atom of carbon 12

Compound:

is formed when the atoms from 2 or more elements combine chemically

The tiny particles that make up the atom are called sub-atomic particles

A proton has a mass of one a.m.u.

A neutron has a mass of one a.m.u.

An electron has a mass of 1/1840 a.m.u.

An Element:

is a simple pure substance which cannot be divided into simpler substances by chemical means

An Atom:

is the smallest particle of an element which can take part in a chemical reaction

A Molecule:

is the smallest part of an element or compound which can exist independently

A molecule can be

  • Monatomic
  • Diatomic
  • Triatomic
Aufbau Pinciple:

electrons always fill the lowest available energy level when the atom is in it's ground state

The electrons revolve around the nucleus in fixed paths called orbits to form an electron cloud

Atoms in their ground state are electrically neutral. This means the number of protons must equal the electrons
